Small and medium B2B companies are chronically over-tooled and under-organized. HubSpot for CRM, Slack for communication, Google Sheets for revenue tracking, email for meeting notes — four different systems that never talk to each other and cost hundreds per month combined.

A Notion B2B operations template consolidates the essential systems into one place — CRM, operations, team, revenue and meeting notes — for a fraction of the cost of separate tools.

What B2B companies need from an operations system

Enterprise tools like Salesforce and HubSpot are built for large sales teams with complex processes. Small B2B service companies — consultancies, agencies, professional services firms — need something simpler that still covers all the essentials. Notion with the right template structure is that sweet spot.

What the B2B Professional Operations template includes

CRM pipeline

Every deal from first contact to closed won or lost. Company, contact, deal value, stage, close date and next action. A deal without a next action and a next date is not a deal — it is a wish. The CRM pipeline makes every deal visible and actionable. Know your pipeline value at any time and forecast next quarter's revenue with confidence.

Operations hub

Every recurring task, project and process — monthly invoicing, quarterly reviews, annual renewals, employee onboarding. Nothing falls through the cracks when everything is logged here with an owner and a due date. The operations hub is the difference between a reactive business and a proactive one.

Team directory

Every team member with role, department, email, skills and current projects. When a new project comes in — check the team directory first to assign the right person based on skills and current workload. As teams grow, this database becomes essential for visibility across the business.

Revenue tracker

Every invoice — retainers, project fees, hourly billing — with status tracked from invoiced to received to overdue. Review every Monday. Chase anything overdue that day. Cash flow problems start with late invoices that go unchased for weeks.

Meeting notes

Every client and internal meeting with attendees, key decisions and action items. Write action items during the meeting — every item needs an owner and a due date. Without both it will not get done. Meeting notes without action items are just conversation — make every meeting count.
