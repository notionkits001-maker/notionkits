What a great freelance Notion template includes

1. Client CRM

The foundation of any freelance business is knowing who your clients are, what they owe you and when to follow up. A good client CRM in Notion tracks every client's status (prospect, active, completed), contact details, total earnings and your next action. No more forgetting to follow up or losing track of who you were supposed to email.

2. Project pipeline

Every project should move through clear stages — from proposal sent to deposit paid to in progress to delivered to paid. A Kanban board view in Notion lets you see every project at a glance and drag cards between stages as work progresses. This alone replaces tools like Trello or Asana for most freelancers.

3. Invoice tracker

Late payments are the #1 frustration for freelancers. An invoice tracker in Notion logs every invoice — amount, client, issue date, due date and payment status. Filter by "overdue" every Friday and you'll never chase a payment late again. This replaces FreshBooks or similar tools for most solo freelancers.

4. Time log

If you bill by the hour, tracking time accurately is essential. A time log in Notion lets you record hours per project as you work, so invoicing is based on real data rather than memory. Link entries to your project pipeline and client CRM for a complete picture of where your time goes.

5. Task board

Daily tasks linked to real projects — not just a generic to-do list. A good freelance task board shows you exactly what needs doing today, linked to the client and project it belongs to, with priority levels so you always work on the right things first.

"The right Notion template doesn't just organize your business — it shows you exactly what to work on, who owes you money and what's coming next. All in one place."
