Most people have no idea where their money goes every month. They earn a decent salary, pay their bills, and somehow end up with nothing left at the end of the month wondering what happened. The problem isn't income — it's the lack of a system to track it.

A Notion personal finance template gives you complete visibility over your money — budget, income, expenses, savings goals and subscriptions all in one place. No complicated spreadsheets, no expensive apps, no monthly fees.

Why Notion works for personal finance

Most budgeting apps are either too simple (just expense tracking) or too complex (full accounting software). Notion hits the sweet spot — flexible enough to track everything you need, simple enough to actually use every day.

The key advantage is customization. Your financial life is unique — Notion lets you build exactly the system that fits your income sources, spending categories and savings goals.

What a great personal finance Notion template includes

1. Monthly budget planner

Set a budget for every spending category at the start of the month. Track actual spending as you go. See instantly where you're over or under budget. Housing, food, transport, entertainment, savings — every category planned and tracked. The moment you see you've spent $267 on dining out against a $200 budget, you change your behaviour for the rest of the month.

2. Income tracker

Log every income source — salary, freelance work, passive income, dividends. Track gross and after-tax amounts. See your total monthly income at a glance. Most people only think about their salary but have 2-3 other income sources they never add up. Seeing all your income sources in one place is often surprising and motivating.

3. Expense log

Every purchase logged with category, date, amount and payment method. The discipline of logging every expense — even small ones — changes your relationship with money. When you know you have to write down that $6.80 coffee, you think twice about it. Mark business expenses as tax deductible and you have your tax records sorted automatically.

4. Savings goals tracker

Every financial goal — emergency fund, holiday, house deposit, new laptop — tracked with target amount, current amount, monthly contribution and progress percentage. Seeing your emergency fund at 56% and your Japan trip at 30% every week keeps you motivated and on track. Goals without tracking are just wishes.

5. Subscription manager

List every subscription with amount, billing cycle and next payment date. Most people are paying for 3-5 subscriptions they forgot about. The average person wastes $30-80 per month on unused subscriptions. One review of this database pays for the template many times over.

The monthly finance routine that works

The template only works if you use it consistently. Here's the minimum routine that makes a real difference:

  • 1st of month — set your budget for every category
  • Every 2 days — log expenses while you remember them
  • When paid — log income and transfer savings immediately
  • Last day of month — review budget vs actual, adjust next month
  • Quarterly — review subscriptions and cancel unused ones
