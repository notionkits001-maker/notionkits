Best Notion Template for Students in 2026

Published June 2026 · 5 min read · NotionKits.co

University is overwhelming. You're managing multiple subjects, dozens of assignments, exam schedules, lecture notes, group projects and a social life — all at once. Most students try to track everything in their head, a calendar app and a notes app that never talk to each other.

Notion has become the most popular productivity tool among university students because it replaces all of those separate tools in one place. The right student Notion template gives you a complete academic system from day one — without spending weeks building it yourself.

Why students love Notion

Notion is free for students and powerful enough to handle everything university throws at you. Unlike rigid apps like Google Calendar or Apple Notes, Notion lets you build databases, link information together and see your workload from different angles — by subject, by deadline, by priority.

The challenge is that most students start with a blank Notion page and don't know how to structure it. A good student template solves that completely.

What a great student Notion template includes

1. Assignment tracker

Every assignment logged with subject, type, due date, priority and status. Sort by due date to see what's coming up this week. Filter by subject to see your workload per course. Mark as done when submitted and log your grade when it comes back. Never miss a deadline again.

2. Exam preparation tracker

For each upcoming exam — subject, exam date, topics to cover, study hours needed, hours already done and a confidence score out of 10. Study the subjects you're least confident in first. Confidence below 5/10 means start immediately. This page alone is worth the cost of the template.

3. Study session log

Log every study session — subject, duration, location, focus score and topics covered. Review notes within 24 hours of a lecture for maximum retention. See which subjects you're actually spending time on versus which ones you're avoiding. Data beats guesswork every time.

4. Class notes database

Organized notes with key concepts and action items from every lecture. Write the action items during the lecture — what do you need to review, read or practice? Do these within 24 hours while the content is fresh. This habit alone can transform your grades.

5. Weekly schedule

Your entire timetable — lectures, study blocks, breaks and social time — in one view. Schedule study time before your week starts, not "whenever I have time." Scheduled study happens. Unscheduled study doesn't.
